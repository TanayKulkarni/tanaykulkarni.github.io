Demand forecasting for COVID-antibody treatment - Eli Lilly and Company



Eli Lilly had recently launched their single COVID antibody treatment where they found out that their treatment was safe and can also prove very useful for high risk individuals which they classified as the elderly and the obese.

The project aimed at giving insights to the Global Supply Chain, Manufacturing and Quality areas of their business about the future demand for their COVID-anitbody treatment globally and also for the high-risk individuals.

The project included the use of different machine learning algorithms in the area of time-series forecasting to forecast the demand for their new antibody shots.

It also included capturing different patterns and seasonalities in the COVID-19 cases and delivering demand predictions specifically for the elderly and the obese.

Customer reaction analysis using emotion recognition model


One of the drawbacks of the traditional feedback systems is that they cannot provide you with accurate on the go feedbacks, most customers either don't recollect their exact opinion about a particular product or they just pass giving feedbacks most of the times.

This gives the companies a hard time in analyzing the areas of improvements in a particular product or understanding the exact customer needs.

The mere thought that what if the customer’s reaction for a particular product is analysed and the corresponding emotion is mapped to the reaction to get a comprehensive customer review on a particular product drove the project.

The project aims at integrating the facial emotion recognition model and an aspect-based LSTM model for Natural Language Processing to get a comprehensive and reliable emotion recognition model.

This is an example of a feedback given for a laptop during a laptop purchase.

This video shows how the model processes the reaction.


The scope of the project can be extended to many areas of business where there are constant customer-product interactions and can also prove to pivot sales strategies for the companies in a better direction.

Recommendation System for E-learning portal



One of the common drawback of E-learning systems is that the content in these systems is not customized to the individual learnability of the users.

The thought of having an E-learning system that could recommend content to the students based not only on their individual learning competences but also according to the content that users with similar level of learninability have interacted with, drove this project.

The project first used both explicit and implicit user profiling techniques to segregate users according to their areas of preferences, interests and behavior and pooled them accordingly.

Further, the techniques of content-based recommendations and user-based recommendations were used combined as a hybrid system, which enabled the system to recommend the content to the user according to the content similarity in user activity history and also the user similarity with other peers in the same user pool.

The acclimatization of E-learning systems is reflected in the ways of how efficiently students can understand the learning materials, how well the methods of communication and interaction suit them and whether the recommended content really serves the purpose of the students. The use of Hybrid filtering increases the quality of recommendations given to the students and thus helps them increase their adaptiveness to the system.

Supply Chain using Blockchain for a supply chain company - SKF Technologies India Pvt Ltd


SKF Technologies India has a huge manufacturing plant in Pune, India. One of the prominent challenges that they were facing is getting an accurate estimate on lead time for component production at their plant and getting a comprehensive end-to-end view of their supply chain.

This challenge not only had effects on their component delivery but also on the raw material supply that they ordered for component production.

The project included forecasting the demand from stakeholders further down in supply chain and estimating the date of order for a particular component which in turn gave an estimate raw material ordering date for the company to keep their production lines running and maintain an optimum inventory level.

The project also integrated the use of blockchain in the company's supply chain from the raw material manufacturer to the end customer to achieve the major goals of transparency, security and immutability throughout the supply chain for all stakeholders.
